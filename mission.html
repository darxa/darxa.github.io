Stitching it together

Our scalable and state of the art end-to-end solution helps secure your region. We take it as a game, where we have rangers and the region to secure. The rangers are equipped with a handheld device with D.A.R.X.A AI application with real-time analysis updates from a control center. The GPS locations of each ranger's handheld device are known to Phoenix-T and the control center. If Phoenix-T detects any human signature other than the ranger's GPS location, that's an Alert! The network of TRACKODS installed at strategic positions over the region sends all the data to the control center. The TRACKODS communicate with each other keeping Phoenix-T airborne and helps the control center to remotely control Phoenix-T. The control center can remotely send commands to TRACKODS to maneuver Phoenix-T as well as update the handheld devices in real-time. The height of the TRACKODS is adjustable which gives us the flexibility to monitor from ground level as well as have a bird's eye view. Everything on TRACKODS and Phoetix-T are remotely accessible and that's powerful!

We train our algorithms on past poaching/ trespassing data of a certain region based on which it can predict GPS locations of probable future happenings. We create GPS waypoints of these hotspots as well as GPS locations of animal herds where Phoenix-T can routinely maneuver to keep a check. Rangers can call Phoenix-T to certain GPS locations via his handheld device to keep a check of a certain region. All the sensor data and imagery data from Phoenix-T are transferred to the TRACKODS, TRACKODS adds all it's data and sends them all together to the control center via a fast wired connection.

We 3-D map the whole region and know the shortest routes between certain GPS points. The rangers are visible over the 3-D mapping with their GPS locations. In case of any Alert! on a specific GPS location, the shortest path to that particular location from each ranger is shown in the handheld device. Everything is monitored and mapped in the control center with real-time visual analysis on the 3-D map of the region on a big screen. The control center can send Phoenix-T to certain GPS locations, alert the rangers and have complete remote control of Everything. The ranger's handheld devices are equipped with additional features to keep them secure and aware.
